What is TSA?
Overview
TSA, or Technology Student Association, is an organization focused on developing skills for working in technical careers based on STEM principles. Across the country, middle and high school teams compete in conferences ranging from regional, state, and national levels. Our chapter upholds a proud legacy, having won consecutive state and national championships for nearly a decade. As fierce competitors, skilled craftsmen, and dedicated teammates, we welcome you to BRHS TSA.
The Creed
I believe that Technology Education holds an important place in my life in the technical world. I believe there is a need for the development of good attitudes concerning work, tools, materials, experimentation, and processes of industry. Guided by my teachers, artisans from industry, and my own initiative, I will strive to do my best in making my school, community, state, and nation better places in which to live. I will accept the responsibilities that are mine. I will accept the theories that are supported by proper evidence. I will explore on my own for safer, more effective methods of working and living. I will strive to develop a cooperative attitude and will exercise tact and respect for other individuals. Through the work of my hands and mind, I will express my ideas to the best of my ability. I will make it my goal to do better each day the task before me, and to be steadfast in my belief in my God, and my fellow Americans.
More Than A
Club
Dedication
Braden River High School’s Technology Student Association is dedicated to training innovators, designers, and inventors to lead in the technical world. Through competitive events, our chapter builds a tight-knit community focused on innovation, collaboration, integrity, and excellence. Our focus is beyond trophies, titles, and accolades. Our mission is to prepare our members for the rapid advancement of technical careers, building a foundation for our leaders of tomorrow.
Values
Work Ethic
Our chapter’s success is founded upon the incredible work ethic of our members. We are not merely a club; we are a competitive team. To remain competitive, we require that our members dedicate their time and effort needed for high-quality projects.
Character
Braden River High School TSA has always prioritized character over competition. We pride ourselves in displaying important aspects of character, including professionalism, work ethic, responsibility, and respect. Our chapter does not tolerate unprofessional behavior, and we hold high expectations that our members actively represent our high moral standards.
Scholarship
We have high academic standards, and members of our chapter are required to meet these standards. Braden River TSA highly values academic achievement, so we require that our members maintain a strong academic record to continue competing.
Competitions
Braden River High School prides itself for its communication between team leads and chapter officers. After many regional, state, and national teams, our club has established a network where chapter officers focus on leading the entire chapter itself, while the team leads are responsible for all the team members and assignments that are specific to their own event. This efficient system then allows the officers and advisors to gain information on the work ethic of the members, and interfere if an important project has not been finished on time. Our chapter chooses to not only compete with other high school teams, but with each other in order to grow our organization and aspire to be the best in the country.
LEAP
LEAP (Leadership. Education. Achievement. Personal Growth) is the Technology Student Association’s leadership program that encourages each member to take every opportunity to become the best member they can be. LEAP is divided into three pillars and is integrated into competitions to effectively develop students into the leaders of their generation. As leaders of the technological future, BRHS TSA incorporates LEAP into all that we do.

Events
Members may compete in 37 different TSA competitive events. Each event focuses on a particular field of study, like biotechnology or coding, and can be an individual or team events. For several months, members construct projects for their events to submit at conferences and be judged and compared to other submissions from around the region, state, and nation.
Conferences
In TSA, chapters attend conferences throughout the year to compete in events and build connections and skills. Projects are submitted at the regional, state, and national level for competition against other high school chapters. Members have the opportunity to experience a competitive environment, meet new people, and travel to remarkable locations for unforgettable experiences.
Meetings
Chapter meetings are mandatory and allow members to communicate and work together. The meetings are held on Tuesdays and Wednesdays from 2:30 pm - 4:30 pm. During each meeting, chapter officers announce any news or upcoming events, and then proceed with individual event team meetings. Members meet with other students on their events to work and discuss projects.
